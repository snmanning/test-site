A Symbol of Hope

Then I said: 'I shall die with my nest, and I shall multiply my days as the phoenix;' (Job 29:18, Jewish Publication Society Bible of 1917)

The phoenix is an evoking image for the abuse survivor because:

  1. a phoenix lives its life in consuming fire, like a victim being abused
  2. a phoenix eventually succumbs to the flames, dying
  3. a phoneix, once dead, will arise anew from its own ashes - evidence of a broken past
  4. but now, though on fire again, these are the flames of passion for a life renewed
